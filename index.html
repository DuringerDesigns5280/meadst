Welcome To Mead St.

Mead Street Station is a family-friendly restaurant and bar located in the heart of the Highlands focusing on scratch-made American food and handcrafted cocktails. Our casual atmosphere makes it the perfect place to come enjoy nearly any professional or college sporting event, have a dinner with the family, or imbibe in drinks with friends. This restaurant was established in 1994 and has been owned and operated by the Burke Family since June 6, 2011. From the kitchen to the bar we source much of our product from Colorado and concentrate on providing fresh, seasonally rotating options on our menu.

We can’t wait to see you at the station!

Craft Cocktails

Speciality Cocktails
Our specialty cocktails are carefully selected and crafted with our wide range of clientele in mind—from sweet to dry, simple to complex we have something that will please everyone.
Whiskey Selection
If whiskey is your drink of choice your palette will feel at home at the Station. With a selection of over 50 varieties and a staff that is given frequent trainings from local and national distillers our knowledge and options will keep even the most discerning of taste buds happy.
Happy Hour
Everyone deserves a little break from the work week and that is why Mead Street Station provides a happy hour every Monday-Friday from 3pm-6pm. We have six select drafts for only $3 a pint, (5 of which are native to Colorado), our well liquors are $4, and we offer a red or white house wine option for $5. Our snack menu includes two different kinds of poutine, house-smoked wings, and al a carte fish tacos—the perfect portions for a mid-afternoon pick-me up.
Beer
We keep the taps at the bar in heavy rotation to keep up with the ever-changing world of craft beer. Denver is known for its love of all things brewed and Mead Street concentrates on providing our customers with the latest and greatest tap options sourced locally, nationally, and internationally. Our menu changes with the seasons so whether you’re looking for a stout to help you warm up in the winter, a sour to cool you in the summer, or something spicy and hoppy while the leaves are changing we have you covered.
